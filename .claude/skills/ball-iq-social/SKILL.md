# Ball IQ: social, conversion, and the funnel

## The account

**@shithouseryhq** — a football banter *brand*, not Alex's personal voice. Bio: "Football News • Commentary • Banter."

| | Followers | Views (30d) |
|---|---|---|
| Instagram | 30.4K | 16.7M |
| Threads | 25.3K | 19.1M |
| X (**separate identity**) | 45K | — |
| TikTok | 17K | — |

Ball IQ has **~35 organic players ever**. A pinned Threads post did 26.5K likes / 150 comments / 307 reposts. The reach is real and enormous.

## ⚠️ Be honest about the numbers — Alex will catch you

**Do not compute "36M views ÷ 35 users = 0.0001% conversion."** It's rhetorically punchy and analytically wrong. Alex pushed back on exactly this on 2026-07-15 and he was right:

> *"a small fraction of the monthly views will visit the profile and a fraction of that again reads the bio so we need to be somewhat more realistic"*

**"Views" are post impressions.** They are not people, not profile visitors, and not bio readers. The real chain is:

```
post impression → profile visit → bio read → link tap → page load → first play → day-2 return
```

## STOP ESTIMATING — the real funnel is measured

Alex pulled Instagram Insights on 2026-07-15. **Do not model this funnel; these are the actual numbers:**

| Step | Count (IG, 30d) | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Views | 16,700,000 | — |
| Profile visits | 24,746 | **0.148%** of views |
| **Bio link taps** | **20** | **0.081%** of profile visits |

**Twenty.** Typical bio-link CTR is 1–5%; this is 12–60× below that.

**Interactions by content type (IG, 30d):** Posts **883K** · Reels **11K** · Stories **267** · Live 0.

### What this means, and the estimating lesson

**883K post interactions → 20 bio taps is 44,000:1.** This audience engages enormously and converts ~zero. That is the defining property of a banter/meme audience — they came for the joke and they scroll on. **Reach here is not demand.** Do not treat the two as the same quantity.

Three successive estimates were made on 2026-07-15 and **all three were too high**: "19.1M → 19,000 users", then (after Alex's first correction) "hundreds to low thousands", then reality: **20**. Each time Alex caught it. The lesson is not "estimate more conservatively" — it is **don't estimate when the instrument exists**.

**The headroom case (real, but bounded):** those 20 taps came from a bio reading *"Get our Football Trivia App for FREE"* pointing at the **Norwegian** App Store — an actively repellent CTA, not merely weak. Reaching even 1% (still below typical) = ~250 taps/month; 2% = ~500. **500 new players/month against 35 organic players ever is transformative for Ball IQ** — but it is hundreds, not thousands. Never reach for the bigger number again.

**⚠️ Open experiment (started 2026-07-15):** the Threads bio was rewritten to point at `balliq.app/footle` this day. **Check IG/Threads "Bio link taps" ~2026-07-22.** Still ~20 → the bio was never the constraint and this audience genuinely doesn't click (hard, useful fact). 200+ → the door was shut and now isn't. **Read the number before theorising further.**

**⚠️ Untested speculation, flagged as such:** a claim was made this day that "IG Story link stickers are the strongest surface because they skip the profile-visit step." Mechanically plausible — but **Stories show only 267 interactions**, i.e. Alex barely posts them, so there is *no evidence either way*. Do not present it as a finding.

**Where the instruments are:**
- **Threads → Insights tab** (profile views, follows).
- **Instagram → Professional dashboard → Insights** — views, profile visits, **bio link taps**. This is the source of the table above.
- **Vercel Analytics** — live since 2026-06-27, `/_vercel/insights/script.js` returns 200. Referrers panel never opened. Hobby tier retains ~1 month.

## The core mechanic: the app is never the subject of a post

Threads/IG/TikTok run promo classifiers and they are good — off-platform links, "link in bio", app screenshots, "download", "FREE" all get throttled, because that reach is meant to be *bought*. This is durable and economic, not a fad: suppressing free distribution is how the platform sells ads.

**So don't post ads. Post football.** A hard football question as plain text has nothing to detect. It's indistinguishable from his normal content because it *is* his normal content. The question isn't an ad for the quiz — the question *is* the quiz. He has 4,145 of them with written explanations: an infinite content well.

Proven exemplar (posted 2026-07-15):

> Brazil lost 7-1 to Germany. Everyone remembers that.
>
> Nobody remembers who scored the 1.

**Why it works:** it's cruel, it's a joke with a fact inside, and **it does not ask.** It asserts something mildly insulting about the reader, whose brain involuntarily objects *"…I might, actually."* Cunningham's Law — people correct a wrong-sounding claim faster than they answer a question.

### Post rules (hard)

- **No CTA in a post. Ever.** Alex proposed "do you know?" — wrong, and the reasoning matters: a question can be *ignored*; a claim that you don't know something demands rebuttal. It also converts an assertion into a quiz (the frame being hidden) and is the most recognisable brand-account tell. **If a post needs anything, it needs less.**
- No emojis, hashtags, "Did you know", "Fun fact", quiz-show framing, "comment below".
- **Never reuse a skeleton.** "Everyone remembers X. Nobody remembers Y." works **once**. Run it weekly and the audience stops reading it as a thought and reads it as a *format* — and a format is a brand account. Vary the shape every time.
- **Press photo fine; designed question-card never.** The former is football content; the latter announces itself as marketing before a word is read. The account is image-led, but the Brazil post is a deliberate **text-only control** — read Insights before concluding either way.
- **Never self-reply on a timer.** Alex's pushback, and he was more right than he said: **the unanswered question IS the funnel.** Someone who doesn't know has an itch and the nearest scratch is the bio link. Answering it scratches it for free and guarantees no click. **Instead reply to whoever gets it right first** — rewards the engager, confirms without resolving the post for later arrivals, and a reply is invisible to classifiers.

### Post rules ≠ bio rules

**This distinction was missed on 2026-07-15 and Alex caught it.** The "no CTA" logic is about *posts*: you're interrupting someone and an algorithm is judging you.

**A bio is neither ranked nor an interruption.** The reader already chose to be there — they came looking. **A CTA belongs in a bio, and a pointer (👇) is doing a job, not begging.** A bio that describes the game without pointing at the link leaves the reader to infer the connection. Alex flagged exactly this:

> *"it just says footle play it in browser but it does not feel obvious that the link below is what is being referred to?"*

He was right. Deictic pointers are functional in bios. What was wrong with the *original* bio was "app"/"FREE" and doubled 👇👇 — not the concept of pointing.

## Link surfaces, ranked by actual power

**The bio is a weak funnel** — it only catches people who already visited the profile, which is a small fraction of impressions. Better surfaces exist and are unused:

1. **Instagram Story link sticker — the strongest, and used ONCE.** Stories appear directly in followers' trays and **skip the profile-visit step entirely**: one tap from story to game. This is the shortest funnel available and it's daily-repeatable. The obvious play: post the daily Footle grid to Story with a link sticker.
2. **Story Highlights** — permanent, on-profile. **An "Our APP" highlight already exists** on Instagram. It points at the app; it should point at Footle.
3. **Bio link** — only converts profile visitors. Now fixed on Threads.
4. **Reply when asked** — when someone asks "where are these from", answering is invisible to every classifier. Free.
5. **Posts** — cannot link, don't try.

## Friction is the whole game

The old CTA asked someone who just laughed at a football joke to: leave the platform → open the App Store → (on the **Norwegian** storefront) → download 50MB → create an account → onboard → play. Each step sheds most of what's left. That's why it converted at ~nothing.

**Footle plays in the browser in three seconds and requires nothing.** The app is the upsell for people who already like it — never the front door.

**Destination: the short attributed redirects. Never the App Store.**

| Link | 307s to | Use on |
|---|---|---|
| `balliq.app/t` | `/footle?utm_source=threads` | Threads |
| `balliq.app/ig` | `/footle?utm_source=instagram` | Instagram |
| `balliq.app/tt` | `/footle?utm_source=tiktok` | TikTok |
| `balliq.app/x` | `/footle?utm_source=x` | X |

Added in `vercel.json` (`redirects`, commit `d4535fb`, 2026-07-15) because **`balliq.app/footle?utm_source=threads` renders TRUNCATED on a Threads profile** — it displays as `balliq.app/footle?utm_source`, which reads as a malformed URL on the one element you want tapped. Alex spotted it; his earlier "we probably want a relatively short link" instinct was right, just for a display reason rather than the save-failure we were debugging at the time.

Why it works: Vercel processes `redirects` **before** `rewrites`, so these resolve before the `/((?!assets/).*)` → `/index.html` catch-all swallows them. They are 307 not 301 — targets may change and browsers cache 301s indefinitely. Query merging works (`/t?cb=1` → `/footle?cb=1&utm_source=threads`).

**Deploys propagate per-edge:** immediately after pushing, `/t` returned 307 on one check and 200 seconds later on the next — different Vercel edge nodes, mid-rollout. Poll several times before concluding a redirect is broken.

The raw form (`balliq.app/footle?utm_source=<platform>`) is still correct anywhere display doesn't matter.
- `/footle` is a boot alias (`src/App.jsx:8313`) and **preserves the query string** (`:8314`) — UTMs survive. Verified.
- Verified 2026-07-15: cold arrival works. Logged out, phone viewport, tapped link → straight into a playable grid. No onboarding wall, no signup gate.
- Footle is the product, not the trivia app: it owns every long streak; 8 users have played it 9–22 separate days.

### Attribution

- **Use a distinct `utm_source` per surface** (`threads`, `instagram`, `instagram_story`, `tiktok`). This is what tells you which platform actually converts vs which merely *feels* big.
- Meta wraps outbound links in redirectors (`l.instagram.com`, `l.threads.net`) which can strip the referrer — **so UTM is the reliable signal, not referrer**.
- Vercel Analytics reads both. IG's own dashboard reports link clicks natively.

## The daily-game playbook

Wordle spread with **zero advertising**. The mechanism was the **share grid**: native text, no link, brags rather than sells — someone posting a score is showing off, not advertising, so it slips every classifier and reads as social proof.

Ball IQ already emits one (`src/App.jsx:4570`, `:6886`) with `balliq.app/footle` appended. **It has never worked**, because until 2026-07-15 every share card unfurled blank (`robots.txt` blocked `/api/og`). This is the single sharpest weapon available and it is newly functional and completely untested.

Also durable and worth knowing:
- **Short-form video reach is largely follower-independent** (TikTok, Reels). 17K TikTok followers doesn't cap a good "guess the player" video — the algorithm doesn't care. Highest ceiling, highest effort, and Alex doesn't use it.
- **Saves and shares outrank likes** as ranking signals almost everywhere. A post worth arguing about beats a post worth liking.
- **Don't scale a funnel before it converts.** Posting 3×/day multiplies the top of a funnel; if the bottom leaks, 3×0 is still 0. Fix the destination, prove one post works, *then* scale. Volume was never the constraint.

## The fact-check bar: ZERO

Alex, 2026-07-15: *"we can not have wrong questions or answers or anything fabricated in our question bank, absolutely ZERO."* Doubly true for posts — 25.3K football obsessives, real name, on an account whose entire credibility is knowing football.

**Every football fact must come verbatim from `src/questions.js`, cited by `q_id`** — present in the entry's `q`/`o`/`hint`, not merely "true as far as I know." A 2026-07-15 drafting run mined 20 posts; **5 failed fact-check**, 2 were real errors.

- **`a` is an INDEX into `o`, not the answer.** Off-by-one produces a fluent, confident, wrong post. Real near-miss: `q_0b5f8f` has `o:["1","2","3","0"], a:3` — `o[2]` is literally the string `"3"`, matching the index, so a misread yields "3 English managers" instead of **0**.
- **Hints deliberately don't name the answer** — that's what makes them hints. "Hint doesn't mention the key" signals a *good* hint. A check built on that assumption flagged 123 correct questions. **Don't rebuild it.**
- **Editorial voice fine; invented detail not.** "He has never had to buy a drink since" is a joke. "The fifth penalty" when the hint says only "the decisive kick" is unsourced even if true elsewhere.
- **Beware broken sources.** `q_f2d34d` (Bergkamp) asks for a score whose key contradicts the question's plain reading, and the natural answer isn't even an option. `flag:true` — and commit `1fff419` claims to have "triaged all 566 flagged questions", so **flagged ≠ fixed**. 412 still carry `flag:true`.

## Mechanics of posting

Alex authorised posting via the Chrome extension (`mcp__claude-in-chrome__*` — his real browser and session). It works end to end: compose → type → Post → verify on profile.

**Posting is publishing public content: get an explicit yes on the literal text every time.** "Pick one and post it" was per-post, not a standing licence. Editing a live bio is an account-settings change — same rule, and a yes for one account does not cover another.

### ⚠️ Threads bio links: DO NOT press the outer "Done"

Discovered the hard way 2026-07-15 after reporting a link live **three times** when it wasn't. Alex caught it every time.

**The Links sub-panel commits immediately. The outer `Done` on the Edit profile sheet then re-submits the whole profile with a stale links array — silently reverting your changes.** No error, no warning: the UI shows your change, a reload shows it gone. It clobbers even when the array *looks* clean at sheet-open.

**Correct procedure:**
1. Edit profile → Links → change (add / edit / drag-reorder — **drag works**).
2. `Done` on the *link sub-sheet* only.
3. **Navigate away. Never press the outer `Done`.**
4. Hard-reload and verify.

**The bio field is the opposite** — it *needs* the outer Done. So **edit bio and links in separate passes**, and after any bio save, re-check the links and restore what got clobbered.

**Red herrings ruled out by testing — don't re-derive:** not the query string (`?utm_source=threads` saves fine), not URL length (short `/footle` failed identically), not the drag.

**Threads bio limit is ~150 chars and truncates silently.** Count before typing; "no app needed" shipped as "no app".

## The meta-rule

**A screenshot one second after a click shows the optimistic UI, not the server. Hard-reload before claiming any social edit landed.**

This is the same lesson as `Math.sin` (137/3000 values differ JSC vs V8), `robots.txt` (blocked every OG card while the renderer was flawless), the 8-player MP "verification" (passed via the fallback path), and `curl` returning **200 for `/this-path-does-not-exist-xyz`** because of the SPA rewrite. **The check that feels like verification usually isn't.** Change one variable at a time; that's what found every real bug here.
